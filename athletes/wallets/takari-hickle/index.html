Mike MacIntyre
Defensive Coordinator / Safeties Coach
Scheme Flexible

IMPACT ON HICKLE: Seumalo developing interior push directly benefits Hickle's sack production. In 2025, Hickle had 2 sacks — DT play quality beside him determines whether that number doubles in 2026.
Scheme Fit Analysis
Defensive Scheme Transition — What MacIntyre Will Run
Oregon State under Trent Bray ran a base 4-3 defense. MacIntyre is scheme-agnostic and adapts to the personnel in the building. Given Hickle's size (6'3", 289) and his evolution from a 240-lb edge rusher to a near-290-lb interior/edge hybrid, here's how each potential scheme impacts his role:
If MacIntyre Runs 4-3 Base
Hickle projects as a strong-side DE or 3-technique DT. His 289 lbs gives him enough anchor against the run while his pass-rush ability (4 career sacks) keeps him on the field in passing situations. High fit — this is closest to what he played under Bray in 2025.
FIT: A-
If MacIntyre Runs 3-4 Base
Hickle could play 5-technique DE in a 3-4 front — a two-gap player responsible for controlling the C-gap. His weight gain supports this. Alternatively, he could rush from the OLB position in sub-packages. Moderate fit — requires technique adjustment but his motor and size translate.
FIT: B+
Most Likely: Hybrid 4-2-5 / Multiple Front
MacIntyre's most recent DC tenures (Memphis, Ole Miss) featured multiple fronts that shift between 4-3 and nickel packages. Hickle's versatility — his HS background playing DE, FB, and OL — makes him an ideal fit for a multiple-front defense that asks linemen to play different techniques based on formation. His 2025 stat line (38 tackles, 2 sacks across multiple alignments) already demonstrates this adaptability. BeaversEdge identified Hickle as a projected starter entering spring under this new staff.
