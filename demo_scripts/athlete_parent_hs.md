# HS Athlete + Parent Demo Script

**Audience:** High school athlete (16-18) and at least one parent. Often the parent is the gatekeeper.
**Time:** 8 minutes (6 min walk + 2 min Q&A)
**Open:** `https://glove2044.github.io/edge3-model/#recruiting-tape`
**Tone:** Conversational. Validate first. Educate second. Sell last.

---

## 0:00 — Validate the family (1 min)

> "First — your highlight tape is great. I want to say that up front. Keep posting it. Frame it. Send it to coaches. The work you've put into it is real, and it has a place in your recruiting.
>
> What I want to do today is show you the OTHER half of the conversation that's happening in college coaching offices when they evaluate you. It's the half nobody told you about. And once you see it, you'll understand why some highlight reels get scholarship offers and most don't."

*(Wait for buy-in. Don't move forward until the parent's body language relaxes.)*

---

## 1:00 — The reveal (1.5 min)

*Scroll to the "Highlight tapes aren't wrong. They're just incomplete." section on the main landing.*

> "When a college coach studies an opponent for next week's game, what do you think they watch? Just the touchdowns? The dunks?
>
> They study the third-down stop they couldn't get last year. The pass protection from the running back. The route the receiver ran on the play the ball never arrived.
>
> **They evaluate recruits the same way they evaluate opponents.** And here's the unlock — and this is the line that matters:
>
> *You can score and look bad. You can never touch the ball and show a coach exactly what they want at your position.*
>
> Scoring isn't the bar. The way coaches actually evaluate you is on REPS, not OUTCOMES."

*(Pause. Let it land.)*

---

## 2:30 — What EDGE3 Recruiting Tape is (2 min)

*Scroll the bullet list on the page.*

> "EDGE3 Recruiting Tape is what we build to give coaches the second half. Position-specific clips tied to the traits the program values:
>
> - **A running back's pass-pro reps.** Coaches recruit running backs partly on rushing yards. They DECIDE on running backs partly on whether you'll pick up a blitz when their starter goes down in Week 7.
> - **A shooter's movement off the ball.** Recruiting reels are full of pull-up threes. The actual shooting development is your screening, your spacing, your hot zones.
> - **The extra pass. The recovery. The footwork before the catch.** The plays where you make someone else look good.
> - **An OL's technique on plays where the QB still got hit.** The coach grades the technique, not the outcome.
> - **The gaps still to close.** Coaches WANT to see what you're working on. Honesty signals coachability."

---

## 4:30 — The Wallet + survey layer (1.5 min)

> "The Recruiting Tape pairs with the Athlete Wallet. The Wallet is your story, your stats, your truth. It includes a personalized survey — questions framed by the head coach who's recruiting you, so you're answering honestly to someone who matters, not filling out a generic form.
>
> A few examples of what the survey asks:
> - 'When was the last time you cried because of your sport? What happened?' — that's a resilience question.
> - 'Has a coach ever told you something about your game you disagreed with? What did you do?' — that's coachability.
> - 'What's your honest expectation for what you'll make in NIL during your first college year?' — that's expectation calibration. Coaches need to know if your number matches their offer.
>
> Athletes who answer those honestly stand out. Coaches *know* they're getting the real read. Athletes who answer them dishonestly stand out for the opposite reason."

---

## 6:00 — Specific position examples (1 min — switch on athlete's actual sport)

**If FB athlete:**
> "[Position-specific question from survey, e.g., for a QB:] 'Was your HS offense spread, pro-style, RPO-heavy, or option? How many pre-snap checks did you have?' That's a scheme transition signal — coaches want to know how much they'll need to teach you in Year 1. The honest answer is the right answer."

**If BB athlete:**
> "[Example for a guard:] 'Were you a primary handler, secondary, or off-ball scorer in HS? Did your team run motion offense, ball-screen heavy, or set plays?' That tells the coach how your game translates to their system. The honest answer is the right answer."

---

## 7:00 — Close (1 min)

*Soft, family-room close.*

> "Two questions for you:
>
> 1. **Where are you in your recruiting right now — getting noticed, choosing between offers, or somewhere in between?**
> 2. **What's the biggest worry your family has about the next two years?**
>
> Tell me those two things and I'll tell you exactly what part of the EDGE3 product fits, and what comes first.
>
> One last thing — what we're NOT going to do is tell you to stop making highlight reels. Make them. Post them. Show them to your friends. We turn highlights into scouting video. The two work together."

## Common parent objections + responses

**"How is this different from Hudl / NCSA / our recruiting service?"**
> "Highlight platforms compress what you've already done into a watchable reel — that's a delivery tool, not an evaluation tool. NCSA and similar services are connection services — they help you get on coaches' radar. EDGE3 is the layer that makes the conversation more honest once you're ON the radar. We work alongside both."

**"Why should we trust your survey?"**
> "Three reasons. (1) The questions are framed by the head coach recruiting your athlete — so the trust signal is built in. (2) We don't share survey content with anyone except the coaching staff your athlete is talking to. (3) Athletes who fill it out honestly stand out — and the data shows it."

**"What if my child doesn't want to fill out a survey?"**
> "Totally up to them. We don't push it. But here's what to know: every kid who DOES fill it out is differentiating themselves from the 10 kids who didn't. Coaches notice."

**"What's it cost?"**
> "Athlete Wallet pricing is being finalized. Today the platform is in school-side rollout — coaches use it to evaluate. Athlete-side rollout is the next phase. If you want to be on that early-access list, I'll add you."

**"What about NIL?"**
> "Two things on NIL. (1) The survey asks honest questions about expectation, current revenue, and external pressure. That helps your athlete and the program calibrate the conversation. (2) We don't represent athletes in NIL deals — we surface the context for coaches and families to negotiate honestly."
